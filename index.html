DRY Principle

Why it is really important

how to understand & implement it in a proper way

Dave Thomas          Andy Hunt

Every piece of knowledge must have a single, unambiguous, authoritative representation within a system

- "Many people took it [the DRY principle] to refer to code only: they thought that DRY means “don’t copy-and-paste lines of source.” […] DRY is about the duplication of knowledge, of intent. It’s about expressing the same thing in two different places, possibly in two totally different ways."

  • don't elevate the DRY principle to an absolute

  • it is important to have a good balance between generalizing your intentions and repeating lines of code, because both can create unnecessary complexity

  • DRY is essential and time-saving skill for any coder
